Say “flight-free trip”, and your mind will probably jump to the delicate architecture of Paris or the foodie delights of Brussels. Such city breaks are as easy as pie – or should that be waffles – thanks to a growing roster of direct trains and ferries to some of Europe’s best urban destinations, with [Amsterdam being added to Eurostar’s portfolio only last year](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/travel/destinations/europe/netherlands/amsterdam/articles/amsterdam-eurostar-return-journey-success/).

But flight-free travel shouldn’t only lead to an urban environment. A whole world of charming towns and lush landscapes awaits, even without visiting an airport.

As more people [pledge to give up](https://www.telegraph.co.uk/family/life/forget-plastic-free-february-should-go-no-fly-2019/) – or at the very least reduce – the number of flights they take, it’s heartening to know that wild terrain and hidden coastal towns are still...
